EDITORIAL POLICY

How Meme Pulse selects and maintains archive content

This page explains what the site includes, what it leaves out, and how corrections are handled. The goal is to make the archive useful for visitors before any advertising consideration.

Selection standard

Items are chosen for category relevance, safe-for-work presentation, and usefulness inside a weekly snapshot. The archive favors posts that help show a recognizable trend or recurring joke pattern.

What is excluded

Low-context placeholders, empty category pages, broken media, and adult content are not intended to become part of the curated archive. The site should not publish thin pages purely to expand URL count.

Summary rule

Each item summary is meant to clarify context, not to copy the source platform. Summaries should tell the visitor why the item appears in that category and what signal it represents.

Archive rule

Historical snapshots stay attached to meaningful browsing pages so users can compare changes over time. Older entries should support understanding, not become duplicate low-value landers.

Correction workflow

If a source link breaks or a summary is misleading, visitors should be able to report the issue through the contact page. Corrections should prioritize factual accuracy and navigational clarity.

Advertising principle

Ads should appear on pages that already stand on their own as useful destinations. Informational utility pages are kept light so monetization does not overpower site purpose.
